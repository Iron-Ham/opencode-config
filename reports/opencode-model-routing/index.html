Bottom line

Use Terra xhigh as an interim pilot baseline because Sol cost 1.349× as much per valid result, above the prospectively specified 1.25× ceiling—not because Terra matched Sol's observed quality. The sample is small, iOS-weighted, and produced no Carry-as-is result.

For every reported score, higher is better and rank 1 is best. Quality:Value cubically weights judged quality and then applies cost efficiency. The weighted metric is 60% cubic quality, 25% cost efficiency, and 15% speed efficiency. These are cohort-relative policy weights, not an empirically calibrated utility function; ranks can change when candidates are added. Invalid or noncompliant artifacts cannot win a promotion decision.

02

Production implementation

Workload shape changes the winner.

The final matched gate selects Terra as the interim pilot baseline under the prospectively specified candidate-cost rule while identifying Sol as the stronger measured quality challenger. This is a risk-aware policy—not a universal model ranking.

Current evidence hierarchy

Validation and production handoff come first. Quality, cost, and speed rank only the artifacts that remain viable.

Terra leads the access-control migration, legacy-client permission UI, and admin-capability gating workloads on the weighted metric; GLM leads release orchestration; Sol leads cross-service access IaC and produced the only mergeable artifacts on the iOS comment-identity and Android persisted-thread workloads.

Final matched gate Two repeats per route ran on each of three production-shaped tasks: native editor, native offline/data, and modern client. Every attempt completed; there were no provider terminal failures or benchmark wall-time limits.

Frozen packet provenance Native editor used judge-packet schema v1; native offline/data and modern client used schema v2. Historical judge outputs were not silently regenerated after the harness changed. Final source reconciliation and scoring use the retained outputs under a common decision process; future matched runs use schema v2.

Validity definition A valid result completed the candidate run, passed the declared policy boundary, and passed effective deterministic validation. Invalid artifacts retain blind scores and handoff assessments for diagnosis, but cannot qualify for promotion or the cost-per-valid denominator.

Prospectively specified working rule The thresholds were recorded in the working evaluation log before final adjudication, but no immutable pre-outcome timestamp or content hash was retained. Final decision Use Terra xhigh as the interim pilot baseline because the working rule requires every measured threshold to pass. Sol is the stronger measured quality challenger and remains the explicit premium route; cost is the only failed measured threshold. This does not establish Terra quality parity: neither route produced a Carry-as-is result, and Terra produced no 85-point result. View the privacy-safe final aggregate.

Uncertainty The 4/6 versus 3/6 completion difference is directional, not statistically conclusive: Wilson 95% intervals are 0.300–0.903 for Sol and 0.188–0.812 for Terra. Six of 12 matched artifacts had a judge spread of at least 20 points, so source-reconciled handoff and deterministic evidence outrank panel means.

Boundary sensitivity One Sol offline/data attempt used a denied glob under an external toolchain path. Accepting that read would make Sol 5/6 valid and reduce its cost-per-valid ratio to about 1.079×, clearing the threshold. The current Noncompliant classification follows the frozen attempt-based policy, but that policy materially determines the outcome.

Historical support Across the earlier seven single-attempt workloads, Sol produced 7/7 valid results and 5/7 results at or above 85; Terra xhigh produced 4/7 valid and 3/7 floor-clearing results. The repeated matched gate—not that historical cohort—drives the default decision.

Sampling correction The original seed screen excluded changes outside 30–800 edited lines and 1–20 files. A later 500-merge audit covered only about 24 hours and found that the selected backend, legacy-client frontend, and iOS references skew smaller than their recent domain distributions.

Ten additional workloads have calibrated historical definitions, but candidate trials are complete for only three—Applecart and two iOS tasks. The other seven are runnable references, not model evidence. View the calibration aggregate.

Scope boundary These single-attempt cohorts support workload-specific findings, while the completed repeated switch gate drives the provisional default decision. Representative-workload expansion remains necessary before treating the result as universal. View the historical aggregate.

Coverage boundary Cohort labels name one workload, not an entire platform or work across engineering. The current frontend task is a small legacy-client change, the original iOS route matrix is confined to native comment identity, and the Android shortlist is concentrated in AI chat. Broad routing claims across engineering need broader iOS CRDT, IME, and editor-interaction coverage beyond the completed matched editor/offline tasks, plus query execution, agent-runtime protocols, external-API security contracts, realtime distributed state, complex client/accessibility work, and Android work outside AI.

Open-weight provider frontier

Measured routes, narrow assignments.

GLM 5.2 and Kimi K2.7 Code are selectable through Baseten and Fireworks; DeepSeek V4 Pro is selectable through Baseten. Baseten now has matched outcomes; Fireworks remains unmeasured. The treatment is model + provider + serving path + reasoning setting—not merely a model family name. “DeepSeek V4 Pro” is the retained OpenCode evaluation-route label; comparisons use the recorded provider and exact route identifier because catalog display names can differ.

Baseten GLM's measured input ceiling is 202,720 tokens, not the roughly 1.05M value OpenCode advertised.

Two retained attempts ended after producing substantive patches because the benchmark trusted incorrect catalog metadata. They remain judgeable, but their completion failures are censored as configuration incidents. Managed runs now compact around 182,720 tokens with the 20,000-token reserve.

05

Proposed pilot safeguards

Keep the rollout controllable and auditable.

These are policy recommendations for a controlled shared-default pilot, not claims about a configuration that has already landed.

  1. Developer controlPreserve explicit model overrides, repository-policy precedence, route visibility, and the ability to disable optional roles.
  2. Separate evidenceBuild has a repeated switch gate; Plan has historical iOS-only evidence with a refresh pending; Ultra inherits Build as an unmeasured policy simplification.
  3. Unattended workAvoid short step limits on production controllers while retaining provider-failure and no-progress loop guards plus machine-readable completion evidence.
  4. Advisor consentKeep automatic transcript-fed advice off. Any independent review should be developer-invoked, isolated, read-only, disableable, and reconciled against source and tests.
  5. CompactionLet the active session route compact unless retention evidence justifies a separate, privacy-reviewed compactor.
  6. Open-weight experimentsKeep model, provider, serving path, and reasoning setting pinned and visible; do not route automatically until repeated outcomes exist.
06

Limits and next decisions

Useful now. Designed to be overturned.

  1. No full shipping loop.The implementation cohorts produced and validated patches, but did not complete an edit-build-simulator-CI-release sequence for every route.
  2. Small and uneven production sample.The original 30–800-line, 1–20-file screen excluded tiny and very large work; the later audit covered roughly 24 hours. Only 3/10 calibrated expansion workloads have candidate outcomes. This supports a controlled pilot, not a universal ranking.
  3. The original source-analysis routes did not pass 85.Several implementation artifacts did, but no model substitutes for builds, tests, runtime evidence, independent review, or human ownership.
  4. Incomplete attempts have lower-bound cost.The historical source-analysis Luna xhigh and Terra max figures exclude unfinished future work and are not complete route totals. The separate iOS Terra max attempt is reported as a censored one-hour route with its retained patch and observed cost.
  5. Luna high is workload-sensitive.It led the mixed value frontier and was inexpensive in frontend, but scored poorly in tooling, failed IaC validation, and produced a redrive iOS artifact; keep it bounded.
  6. Planning is historical and incomplete.Terra produced the most stable plans in two iOS clusters under a legacy boundary. Nine refreshed follow-ups are queued, raw per-run artifacts are not public, and the planner-to-implementer effect is unmeasured.
  7. Open-weight evidence is preliminary.Matched Baseten outcomes now exist for several workloads and the ten added references passed calibration, but the full cross-area candidate matrix and provider comparison are incomplete. Vendor token-throughput claims do not establish end-to-end OpenCode speed.
  8. List price is not a bill.Subscription terms, discounts, caching, and regional or long-context rules can change out-of-pocket spend.
  9. Effort remains unresolved.Sol high versus xhigh versus max remains deferred. Terra high beat xhigh on backend and mixed work, while neither effort cleared the original iOS comment task. A three-task Terra max screen could test whether max bridges the quality gap without assuming it does.
  10. No carry-as-is parity.None of the 12 matched attempts cleared source reconciliation without repair. The gate measures relative route performance, not proof that either route can replace production verification.
  11. Judge means are noisy.Six of 12 matched artifacts and 19 of 74 judged production artifacts had a panel spread of at least 20 points. Source-reconciled handoff and deterministic evidence outrank raw means.
  12. Completion is directional.The 4/6 versus 3/6 gate follows a locked policy rule, but its Wilson intervals substantially overlap.
  13. The boundary policy changes the outcome.Accepting one Sol external-toolchain glob would move its cost-per-valid ratio below the threshold. The retained classification is correct under the frozen attempt-based policy, but the sensitivity is material.
  14. Isolation was policy-audited.Historical workspaces and command audits were retained, but network and shell boundaries were not technically sealed.
  15. Advisor evidence is aggregate-only.The arithmetic is internally consistent, but raw prompts, outputs, and judge artifacts are not attached. It supports a conservative no-automatic-advice policy, not a universal claim about independent review.
