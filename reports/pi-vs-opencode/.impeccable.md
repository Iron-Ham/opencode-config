# Design context

## Audience

An expert software engineer choosing the default implementer and escalation models for an overwhelmingly iOS/Swift workload in a very large monorepo.

## Product character

Editorial engineering decision memo: calm, evidence-first, compact, and opinionated. The page should feel like a well-designed technical journal rather than a SaaS dashboard.

## Visual direction

- Warm paper background, near-black ink, cobalt decision color, and restrained green/orange evidence accents.
- Newsreader for editorial display type and Manrope for interface/data text.
- Strong rules, numbered sections, tabular comparisons, and one small quantitative benchmark graphic.
- Avoid card grids, glass effects, gradients, oversized marketing copy, and decorative imagery.
- Responsive down to a narrow phone viewport, with accessible focus states, semantic tables, reduced-motion support, and useful print output.

## Content priorities

1. Lead with Luna xhigh as the low-cost default for routine iOS implementation.
2. Preserve Sonnet, Terra, Ultra, and Sol as clear escalation lanes.
3. Make measured quality, cost, and latency tradeoffs immediately legible.
4. Keep advisor lift and failure risk explicit without dwelling on test machinery.
5. Treat OpenCode as settled context and keep implementation details out of the narrative.
