Production controller

Cheap incomplete work is not cheap.

Two production-shaped source-analysis clusters measured completion, boundary compliance, blind quality, latency, and normalized cost. Three blind graders scored each workload.

Within-experiment

Terra was 24.6% faster. Its complete cost was 8.4% above Luna's incomplete lower bound.

The lower per-token price did not produce the lower-cost valid result for these workloads. No route cleared the locked 85-point quality floor.

Task-class transfer The operational gap supports Terra xhigh as the production iOS controller. Its 80.88 mean does not establish production sufficiency, and the experiment did not run a complete edit-build-simulator-CI-shipping cycle.

Effort and premium lanes

Luna high appeared in one matched cluster. It completed within policy at 73.42 for $0.849. Luna xhigh scored 73.00 in the same cluster, crossed the boundary, took more than twice as long, and incurred at least $1.581. That is directional support for Luna high as the bounded lane—not broad quality parity with Terra.

Terra max had one timed-out attempt at a cost of at least $5.538. This narrow result supports leaving it out of the default routes; it does not show that max effort is never useful.

Sonnet was faster and cheaper but repeatedly omitted central safety constraints. Sol produced two excellent plans, one prohibited plan, and one timeout; its observed total was already 4.9 times Terra's.

Opus 4.8 and Fable 5 received two screening attempts each. Opus scored 45 and 92 at a combined $4.943; Fable scored 15 and 40 at $6.830. Their variance or repeated caps did not justify finalist expansion.

Configuration fact Plan cannot edit files, shell access requires approval, and bounded read/review subagents are available. The absence of a step cap supports long unattended planning; Goal execution remains unavailable from the plan agent.

Advisor causal effect

A modest lift, not an outcome change.

The causal pilot held six Terra drafts fixed across two production-shaped clusters and compared direct output, Terra self-review, and tool-less Sol review followed by Terra reconciliation.

Three matched drafts per cluster; three blind graders per answer
TreatmentMedian delta vs directMean route costMean wall time
Terra direct—$1.1376.3 min

Sol review + Terra reconciliation+2$1.6197.5 min

Sol assistance improved one of six matched drafts by at least five points and harmed none by that amount. It produced no pass of the locked 85-point quality floor and no failed-to-pass rescue.

Median change: +2 over direct, +2.5 over self-review. Cost: +42.35% over direct and +7.16% over self-review.

Policy Automatic transcript-fed review stays off by default. The observed effect is modest and directional, not zero; the policy balances that lift against cost, latency, and the absence of an outcome change.

Unmeasured The pilot did not test the shipped /advise mechanism, which receives only developer-supplied context in an isolated read-only session.

The managed provider and exact model IDs resolve, but no Fireworks credential was present for authenticated trials in this snapshot. There is no defensible Fireworks-versus-Baseten quality, reliability, or realized-latency winner yet.

Configuration fact Bare /glm and /kimi retain Baseten as matched controls. The -fireworks commands pin Standard; -fireworks-fast isolates the premium serving path. Neither hidden agent is reachable through automatic Task routing.

Decision Production Build and Plan stay Terra; the advisor stays provisional Opus; compaction keeps the active model. GLM remains a bounded implementer experiment and Kimi a bounded reader experiment until role-specific repeated outcomes exist.

Fireworks Standard matches Baseten's fresh-input and output rates. It has cheaper GLM cache reads, while Baseten has slightly cheaper Kimi cache reads. Fireworks Fast costs 1.5× Standard for GLM and 2× for Kimi, so it must earn that premium through total model-session time per valid result—not a vendor throughput claim.

06

Deliberate subagent roster

Thin roles, not borrowed authority.

A specialist name is not evidence that delegation improves an outcome. Each retained role has a concrete trigger, an evidence requirement, and a restricted boundary. The controller owns implementation and reconciliation.

General

A substantial independent writable slice with an explicit ownership and file boundary. Only Build and Ultra may invoke it, and it stays on the invoking controller model.

Writable · no recursive delegation, Goal, advisor, interactive question, unknown external tool, or authority-requiring shell action · uncapped by default

Code reviewer

Independent review of a concrete change for material correctness and regression risk. The request must supply a diff or exact changed-file boundary and intended behavior; otherwise the result is unverified.

Read-only · parent supplies diff or exact files · no broad content search or interactive questions

Security engineer

Bounded threat review for an identified trust boundary or consequential change. Separates confirmed vulnerabilities from hardening ideas.

Read-only · parent supplies trust boundary and evidence · no broad content search or interactive questions

Software architect

Challenges consequential cross-boundary decisions against existing invariants, failure modes, and reversible alternatives.

Read-only · parent supplies source and system boundary · no broad content search or interactive questions

Accessibility auditor

Platform-specific review grounded in source or supplied runtime evidence. Missing assistive-technology evidence stays unverified.

Read-only · no broad search or commands · no generic web checklist for native mobile

Database optimizer

Optional analysis of a concrete query, schema, lock, migration, or rollout risk using actual workload evidence.

Read-only · no broad search or execution · an index is never assumed beneficial

Evidence analyst

Independent analysis against an exact claim checklist and already-produced artifacts. The controller runs repository-native verification commands and supplies the results.

No shell, edits, delegation, or interactive questions · absent evidence remains unverified

Explore

Bounded repository discovery used by controllers and the plan route.

Read-only · no shell, edits, delegation, advisor, or Goal

The OpenCode roster retires the generic Mobile App Builder and broad Agents Orchestrator, along with backend, frontend, Git-workflow, and technical-writer personas.

Mobile implementation stays with the production controller plus repository-local mobile instructions and skills. Ultra owns bounded native orchestration. Setup also removes known plugin-generated agents that their source package does not support on OpenCode. Borrowed or emitted role prose is not treated as battle-tested capability.

Configuration fact Retained specialists and explore inherit the invoking primary model by default and have 100-step limits. The writable general child inherits Build or Ultra and is uncapped by default. A Luna controller stays Luna-backed and a Terra controller stays Terra-backed unless the developer explicitly supplies a role-specific local override.

Kimi and GLM remain hidden command-only experiments. Kimi is read-only; GLM is a bounded writable experiment whose shell and broad search require approval. Provider-pinned Baseten, Fireworks Standard, and Fireworks Fast commands reuse those permission boundaries. None is a default because no controlled matched quality, latency, or total-cost comparison supports one. The advisor reviewer is also a hidden subagent: /advise is its only visible entry point, and controllers cannot invoke it through Task.

07

Operational configuration

Long-running, bounded where it matters.

  1. Controller routingBuild, plan, Terra, and Ultra use Terra xhigh. General inherits its invoking Build or Ultra model unless explicitly overridden. Luna uses Luna high; Sonnet uses provider-default Sonnet 5; Sol uses Sol xhigh; explicit advice provisionally uses Opus 4.8 xhigh. Retained specialists and explore also inherit the invoking primary model.
  2. Iteration policyBuild, plan, general, Luna, Terra, Sonnet, Sol, and Ultra have no OpenCode step cap. Explore, specialists, and experiments have 100; the focused reviewer has 60.
  3. Goal safety railsThe build and explicit model controllers can use a 200,000-token default budget, at most 25 automatic continuations, a one-hour cap, and no-progress stops. Ultra explicitly uses Goal for multi-turn work.
  4. Delegation boundaryBuild and Ultra may call writable general, which inherits their model unless explicitly overridden. Model-branded controllers keep implementation in their pinned lane and can call only inherited-model read-only roles. Children cannot recursively delegate or invoke advisor or Goal tools.
  5. Advisor consentThe legacy advisor tool is denied everywhere. The isolated /advise lane is developer-invoked, read-only, configurable, and disableable.
  6. CompactionThe active session model compacts; old tool output is pruned, three recent turns and 12,000 recent tokens are preserved, and 20,000 tokens are reserved. Retention quality is unmeasured.
  7. Open-weight availabilityExact Baseten and Fireworks Standard/Fast commands are selectable, but remain outside default models, small_model, compaction, and automatic Task routing.
  8. Unattended boundarySensitive environment-file reads, broad specialist content search, interactive child questions, unknown external tools, and authority-requiring general-child shell actions are denied.

Developer-owned overrides

Machine-local routing can change the advisor, models, and iteration limits without editing the public defaults.

{
  "advisor_enabled": true,
  "agents": {
    "advisor_reviewer": "anthropic/claude-opus-4-8-xhigh-pinned"
  },
  "steps": {
    "general": 300,
    "code_reviewer": null
  }
}

advisor_enabled controls the explicit review lane. Allowlisted agents entries replace managed models locally. A positive steps value changes a limit; null removes it. Defaults remain a starting point, not centrally locked policy.

08

Limits and next decisions

Useful now. Designed to be overturned.

  1. No full shipping loop.The production confirmation is source analysis, not a complete edit-build-simulator-CI-release run.
  2. Small production sample.Two clusters and one attempt per included route support a directional default, not a broad model ranking.
  3. No route passed 85.Terra is the strongest tested controller, not a substitute for builds, tests, runtime evidence, independent review, or human ownership.
  4. Incomplete attempts have lower-bound cost.Luna xhigh and Terra max figures exclude unfinished future work and are not complete route totals.
  5. Luna high is directional.It appears in one matched cluster and should stay bounded until broader evidence exists.
  6. Planner-to-implementer effect unmeasured.Terra produced the most stable plans; this study did not execute each plan with a cheaper downstream implementer.
  7. Luna plus advisor versus Terra is unmeasured.The advisor studies begin with Terra drafts. They do not show that Luna plus Sol, Sonnet, Opus, or Fable reaches direct Terra quality or lowers cost per valid result.
  8. Advisor transfer is provisional.Opus 4.8 led the two-workload reviewer comparison, but the winning reviewer changed by workload and the benchmarked transcript-fed reconciliation differs from the shipped isolated /advise command.
  9. /advise benefit unmeasured.The shipped isolated mechanism differs from the transcript-fed automatic treatment.
  10. Open-weight outcomes are pending.Fireworks and Baseten prices are normalized, but authenticated matched OpenCode quality, completion, and wall-time trials have not run. Vendor token-throughput claims do not establish end-to-end harness speed.
  11. Subagent outcomes unmeasured.The roster is justified by narrow scope and permissions, not proof that each role improves production results.
  12. List price is not a bill.Subscription terms, discounts, caching, and regional or long-context rules can change out-of-pocket spend.
  13. Sol effort is deferred.High versus xhigh versus max remains a separate experiment.
